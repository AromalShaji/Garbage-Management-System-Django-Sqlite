Organic waste

Method Description
Composting The process of breaking down organic waste into a nutrient-rich soil amendment using a mixture of "greens" (such as fruit and vegetable scraps, grass clippings, and coffee grounds) and "browns" (such as leaves, twigs, and paper).
Vermicomposting Similar to composting, but uses worms to break down the organic waste. This process is done in a worm bin, which is a container filled with bedding material and worms. The worms eat the organic waste and produce a nutrient-rich compost.
Anaerobic digestion The process of breaking down organic waste in the absence of oxygen, which produces biogas that can be used as a source of energy. This process is typically done on a larger scale by industrial facilities.
Landfills Organic waste can be disposed of in a landfill, where it will decompose and release methane gas. However, this is not the most environmentally friendly option, as methane is a potent greenhouse gas.
